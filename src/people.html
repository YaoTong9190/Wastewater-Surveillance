Hyatt Green
Dr. Hyatt Green (Assistant Professor, SUNY-ESF) interested in wastewater detection of SARS-CoV-2, extends directly from his background in microbial source-tracking and the development of molecular markers to help quantify sources of fecal pollution. Parallels also exist between wastewater surveillance and the development and application environmental DNA, or 'eDNA' methods, where he would be able to quantify rare bits of DNA/RNA from difficult substrates.
Brittany Kmush
Dr. Brittany Kmush has PhD in Global Disease Epidemiology and Control from Johns Hopkins School of Public Health. Her areas of specialization include infectious disease epidemiology, vaccine development and application, and maternal-child health. For the past 8 years, she has been focused on the elimination and control of hepatitis in Asia, where it causes substantial maternal mortality.
Dustin T. Hill
Dustin T. Hill(PhD Candidate, SUNY ESF) is an expert in quantitative analysis, data management, and geographic information systems. His background is in environmental policy. His research interests include issues of environmental health inequality, spatial epidemiology, and environmental policy. Dustin is the spatial data manager for this project.
